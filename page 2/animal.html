Animals

noun: any of a kingdom (Animalia) of living things including many-celled organisms and often many of the single-celled ones (such as protozoans) that typically differ from plants in having cells without cellulose walls, in lacking chlorophyll and the capacity for photosynthesis, in requiring more complex food materials (such as proteins), in being organized to a greater degree of complexity, and in having the capacity for spontaneous movement and rapid motor responses to stimulation
- Merriam Webster Dictionary -

  • Mammals

Giant Panda
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Ursidae
Genus Ailuropoda
Spesies A. melanoleuca

Did you know?

A 45-kilo adult (and pandas can reach 150 kg in captivity) spends as long as 14 hours eating. And it can eat 12 to 38 kilos of bamboo a day. Pandas' favorite food is bamboo shoots even though they have carnivorous teeth.

Cheetah
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Felidae
Genus Acinonyx
Spesies A. jubatus

Did you know?

The cheetah is the fastest land animal in the world, reaching speeds of up to 70 miles per hour. They can accelerate from 0 to 68 miles per hour in just three seconds. Cheetahs are the only big cat that can turn in mid-air while sprinting.

South African Giraffe
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Artiodactyla
Family Giraffidae
Genus Giraffa
Spesies G. camelopardalis

Did you know?

Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth. Their legs alone are taller than many humans—about 6 feet. They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances, or cruise at 10 mph over longer distances. A giraffe's neck is too short to reach the ground.

Palm Civet
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Viverridae
Genus Paradoxurus
Spesies P. hermaphroditus

Did you know?

Unlike other species of civet, Asian palm civet does not have rings on the tail. Asian palm civet has big eyes, long, pointed muzzle, small ears, elongated, stocky body and long tail. Asian palm civet is active during the night (nocturnal animal). Asian palm civet is an omnivore.

Asian Elephant
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Proboscidea
Family Elephantidae
Genus Elephas
Spesies E. maximus

Did you know?

Asian elephants are the continent's largest terrestrial mammals. They can reach 6.4m in length and 3m at the shoulder, and weigh as much as 5 tonnes. They have proportionally smaller ears, which they keep in constant motion in order to cool themselves.

Lion
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Felidae
Genus Panthera
Spesies P. leo

Did you know?

Living in the grasslands, scrub, and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa, the lion is the second largest cat in the world. Female lions are the primary hunters of the group. Vulnerable to predators like hyenas, leopards, and black-backed jackals, cubs have a 60-70% mortality rate.

Tiger
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Felidae
Genus Panthera
Spesies P. tigris

Did you know?

The tiger is the biggest species of the cat family. Tigers can reach a length of up to 3.3 metres (11 feet) and weigh as much as 300 kilograms (660 pounds).They are good swimmers and can swim up to 6 kilometres.

Wolf
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Carnivora
Family Canidae
Genus Canis
Spesies C. lupus

Did you know?

Wolves are the largest members of the dog family. Wolves can roam large and long distances, sometimes up to 12 miles (20 kilometers) in a single day. Wolves prefer to eat large animals like deer, elk, and moose. One wolf can eat 20 pounds (9 kilograms) of meat at a single sitting.

Clown Fish
Phylum Chordata
Class Actinopterygii
Order Perciformes
Family Pomacentridae
Genus Amphiprion
Spesies A. ocellaris

Did you know?

A clownfish is born male and has the ability to change into a female. This can happen when the dominant female dies, the dominant male will turn into a female and then choose to mate with another male in the group. However, once they change they cannot turn back.

Dolphin
Phylum Chordata
Class Mamalia
Order Cetacea
Family Delphinidae
Genus Tursiops
Spesies T. truncatus

Did you know?

Dolphin are known for their playful behavior, dolphins are highly intelligent. They are as smart as apes, and the evolution of their larger brains is surprisingly similar to humans. Dolphins are part of the family of whales that includes orcas and pilot whales. Killer whales are actually dolphins.

Peacock Flounder
Phylum Chordata
Class Actinopterygii
Order Pleuronectiformes
Family Paralichthyidae
Genus Paralichthys
Spesies P. dentatus

Did you know?

Male flounders are small, seldom growing larger than 10 to 12 inches, but the females grow larger and move longer distances. Females may reach 23 inches long, and spend most of the year in inshore waters, only migrating offshoreduring October to December to spawn.

Lion Fish
Phylum Chordata
Class Actinopterygii
Order Scorpaeniformes
Family Scorpaenidae
Genus Pterois
Spesies P. volitans

Did you know?

Lionfish are nocturnal and most active during dawn and dusk. Lionfish are noted for their venomous fin spines. Lionfish also called zebrafish, firefish, turkeyfish, tastyfish or butterfly-cod is any of several species of showy Indo-Pacific fishes of the scorpion fish family.

Class Chondrichthyes
Order Rajiformes
Family Myliobatidae
Genus Manta
Spesies M. birostris

Did you know?

Manta Rays have long tails usually have a spine and venom.They also don’t have a bony skeleton. Instead their bodies are made up of the same kind of cartilage in our noses and ears.

Shark
Phylum Chordata
Class Chondrichthyes
Order Lamniformes
Family Lamnidae
Genus Carcharodon
Spesies C. carcharias

Did you know?

Even though sharks don't have bones, they still can fossilize. As most sharks age, they deposit calcium salts in their skeletal cartilage to strengthen it. The dried jaws of a shark appear and feel heavy and solid; much like bone.The teeth have enamel so they show up in the fossil record too.

Killer Whale
Phylum Chordata
Class Mammalia
Order Artiodactyla
Family Delphinidae
Genus Orcinus
Spesies O. orca

Did you know?

Killer Whales Live in Groups Called Pods. Killer whales do not have smelling organs or a lobe of the brain dedicated to smelling, so it is believed that they cannot smell. They do, however, have good senses of sight and hearing. They can hear better than dogs and even bats.
